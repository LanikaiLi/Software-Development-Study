üìú About Luoyang

Luoyang is a historic city located in Henan Province, China. It is one of the oldest cities in China and served as the capital for 13 dynasties throughout Chinese history, spanning over 1,500 years.

Fun Fact: Luoyang is often called the "City of Peonies" and hosts one of China's most famous flower festivals every spring!

‚ú® Key Highlights

  • Historical Significance: Luoyang was the capital of multiple Chinese dynasties, including the Eastern Zhou, Eastern Han, and Northern Wei dynasties
  • Cultural Heritage: The city is home to the famous Longmen Grottoes, a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring thousands of Buddhist statues carved into limestone cliffs
  • Ancient Capital: Known as one of the "Four Great Ancient Capitals of China" along with Beijing, Xi'an, and Nanjing
  • Peony City: Luoyang is famous for its peony flowers and hosts an annual Peony Festival in spring

Luoyang Water Banquet

The most famous local cuisine! A traditional banquet with 24 dishes served in a specific order, mostly soup-based dishes. It dates back over 1,000 years and was once served to emperors.
